## AI Writing Tropes to Avoid

In your writing, you may encounter certain stylistic patterns that are often overused or
misapplied. Below is a list of common AI writing tropes to be aware of and avoid in your
work.

### Word Choice

- **Magic adverbs:** "quietly", "deeply", "fundamentally", "remarkably", "arguably" used to make mundane things feel significant.
- **Delve and friends:** "delve", "certainly", "utilize", "leverage" (verb), "robust", "streamline", "harness".
- **Grandiose nouns:** "tapestry", "landscape", "paradigm", "synergy", "ecosystem", "framework" where simpler words work.
- **Serves-as dodge:** "serves as", "stands as", "marks", "represents" instead of plain "is"/"are".

### Sentence Structure

- **Negative parallelism:** "It's not X — it's Y", "not because X, but because Y",
  "The question isn't X. The question is Y."
- **Not X. Not Y. Just Z.:** Dramatic negation countdown before the actual point.
- **"The X? A Y.":** Self-posed rhetorical questions answered immediately.
- **Anaphora abuse:** Same sentence opener repeated in quick succession ("They assume... They assume... They assume...").
- **Tricolon abuse:** Rule-of-three stacked back to back across multiple sentences.
- **Filler transitions:** "It's worth noting", "Importantly", "Interestingly", "Notably".
- **Superficial -ing tails:** Dangling participles that add no analysis
  ("highlighting its importance", "underscoring its role").
- **False ranges:** "from X to Y" where X and Y aren't on a real scale.

### Paragraph Structure

- **Short punchy fragments:** Sentence fragments as standalone paragraphs for manufactured emphasis ("Platforms do.").
- **Listicle in a trench coat:** Numbered points dressed as prose ("The first wall is... The second wall is...").

### Tone

- **"Here's the kicker":** False suspense before an ordinary point. Also: "Here's the thing", "Here's where it gets interesting".
- **"Think of it as...":** Unsolicited analogies that patronize the reader.
- **"Imagine a world where...":** Futurist preamble used to sell a premise.
- **False vulnerability:** Performative self-awareness ("And yes, I'm openly in love with the platform model").
- **"Let's break this down":** Pedagogical hand-holding for expert audiences. Also: "Let's unpack", "Let's dive in".
- **Vague attributions:** "Experts argue", "Industry reports suggest" — name the source or drop the claim.

### Formatting

- **Em-dash addiction:** Overuse of — for dramatic pauses and asides.
- **Bold-first bullets:** Every list item opening with a bolded phrase.

### Composition

- **Fractal summaries:** Previewing and recapping at every level (section, subsection, document).
- **Dead metaphor:** One metaphor repeated throughout instead of introduced and dropped.
- **One-point dilution:** A single argument restated ten different ways across thousands of words.
- **Signposted conclusion:** "In conclusion", "To sum up", "In summary".
